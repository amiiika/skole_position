Foxes

Foxes make up a large part of the dog family. What is to be considered a fox is a question of definition. Some believe the name should be reserved for species in the genus Vulpes, while others include all species where "fox" has become part of the name. In general, foxes are the smallest and most slender members of the canine family.



































Genus and distribution

There are 23 species of foxes in 4 genera. The genera are: Urocyon: gray fox, 2 species in North America, one of which is widespread south to the northernmost South America. The genus is considered the oldest, it is 4-6 million years old. Otocyon: ear fox, 1 species, O. megalotis, living in Africa. Vulpes: foxes, 12 species, are found naturally throughout the northern hemisphere except the most arctic regions, as well as in Africa. The red fox is perhaps the most widespread mammal in the world. Dusicyon: zorroer, 8 species living in South America. These are also called South American gray foxes. They are more closely related to species in the canine genus Canis than to the other three fox genera.

Description

Foxes are mostly small and slender predators, most species weigh between two and five kg. The smallest is the fennel with 0.8 kg, while the largest is "small ear azure". microtis with 9 kg. They often have large ears, a long, bushy tail and a pointed snout. The coat is mostly shades of gray and brown with white and black spots. The red fox is more reddish, but has a black color variant in the silver fox. The mountain fox also comes in two color variants; white fox and blue fox.

Living

Foxes are typically extremely opportunistic in their way of life. They eat almost everything that is possible to catch and eat where they live, but most prefer small mammals as small rodents. They also eat insects, earthworms, hares, birds, scavengers, fish, fruits and berries, as well as small ungulates (especially young). Where several foxes overlap in distribution, competition for food can lead to variations in food choices and also to changes in body size. The fox has completely specialized in insects, it eats a lot of termites. It uses its large ears as dishes to listen for crawling insects. For foxes in general, both hearing and sense of smell are important in the search for food.
